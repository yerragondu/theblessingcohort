Our story

A family story that became a calling.

Before there was a ministry structure, there was a testimony: hardship, instability, mercy, and the quiet way God prepares people before they can name the work.

God was preparing us long before we understood the assignment.

This mission grew out of a real family journey marked by hardship, instability, survival, and the mercy of God. In many seasons, we did not fully understand what God was doing, but looking back, we can see that He was preparing us for something far bigger than we had imagined.

Through those years, God formed a deep conviction in us: when He calls us to His work, we should respond with faith, responsibility, and obedience rather than hesitation.

Mission statement

Support pastors. Strengthen churches. Reach communities.

The Blessing CoHort exists to support pastors serving in unreached, rural, and remote places, strengthen the local church through them, and reach surrounding communities with the love of Christ through faithful presence, practical care, and Gospel witness.

  • Support pastors serving in hard places
  • Strengthen the local church through them
  • Move outward into communities with care and witness
Our Beliefs

The beliefs shaping the way we carry this work.

This mission is built on biblical convictions that keep Scripture, salvation, the church, and holy living at the center.

  • The Bible is the inspired Word of God and our final authority.
  • There is one God in three Persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.
  • Human beings were created by God but have fallen into sin.
  • Salvation is by grace through faith in Jesus Christ alone.
  • The church is the community of believers called to worship, serve, and proclaim the gospel.
  • Baptism is an outward expression of faith and commitment to Christ.
  • Jesus Christ will return, judge the world, and restore all things.
  • Christians are called to live holy lives, love others, and share the gospel.
  • Family, compassion, and freedom of conscience are deeply valued.
